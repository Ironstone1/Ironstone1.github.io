About Me:

Summary

I am an Early Years Professional with a passion for Special Educational Needs and Disability (SEND). Working within a District Specialist Centre pathfinder, I have been privy to the roll out of the Wiltshire Local Offer and been involved in the implementation of some of the first "My Plans" which combines Education and Health to ensure a holistic approach and joined up services for each child or young person from 0-25 years through the introduction of SEND Lead Workers and the "Single Point of Contact" service. My experience in writing Single-Page Profiles and Person-Centred Planning which is now at the heart of all early years practice, has enabled me to understand the needs of settings and practitioners better so that I can write and deliver training workshops that are current and relevant. "Learning through play" and "Partnership with Parents" are two of the most important messages I like to leave with practitioners and parents, as these underpin a child's well-being and development.

Skills Matrix & Personal Attributes Before finding my element in early years education, I was a Hotelier and Restaurateur. Working within this demanding, fast-paced industry taught me good business skills, time and project management abilities, endurance, resilience, managerial skills, diplomacy and it gave me insight into the impact of socio-economic and cultural differences. I have owned and managed my own restaurant and early years setting, which entailed all the managerial, financial and human resources responsibilities. I have carried these professional skills through to my employment and found them invaluable in working with colleagues, other practitioners and families when providing 1:1 support or facilitating training.

Experiance

I participate in CAF's, Team Around the Child (TAC) and My Plan Meetings, as part of a multi-agency approach to meeting my keychild's needs holistically.
